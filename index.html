Healthy Young Man — A Book Club

Our Story

We're Ibrahim, Marco, and Kevin—three friends who met in 8th grade back in 2015. We grew up together, bonding over robotics in middle school and high school, and eventually all pursuing electrical and computer engineering in university.

Our book club was born from our shared love of books and community. We'd often find ourselves at Barnes & Noble, browsing the shelves, picking out our next reads, and then heading to a local tea shop to enjoy tea and play chess together. It was during these moments that the idea of starting a book club naturally emerged.

We meet every other Sunday to discuss the book we're reading, sharing thoughts, insights, and the joy of discovery. Our first meeting was the first Sunday of November 2025, where we decided on our inaugural book: "The Secret History" by Donna Tartt.

Currently Reading

The Alchemist

by Paulo Coelho

About the Author: Paulo Coelho is a Brazilian lyricist and novelist, born in Rio de Janeiro in 1947. He is best known for his allegorical novel The Alchemist, which has become one of the best-selling books in history, translated into over 80 languages. Coelho's works often explore themes of spirituality, personal growth, and the pursuit of one's dreams. Before becoming a full-time writer, he worked as a theatre director, actor, and songwriter.

About the Novel: The Alchemist is a philosophical novel published in 1988. It follows Santiago, a young Andalusian shepherd who travels from Spain to Egypt in search of a treasure buried near the Pyramids. Along the way, he meets various characters who teach him about following his dreams and listening to his heart. The novel is an allegory about pursuing one's personal legend and finding meaning in life's journey.

Books We've Read

The Secret History

by Donna Tartt

About the Author: Donna Tartt is an American novelist, essayist, and critic. She was born in Greenwood, Mississippi, and attended Bennington College. Tartt is known for her literary fiction that often explores themes of morality, beauty, and the darker aspects of human nature. Her works are characterized by their meticulous prose and psychological depth.

About the Novel: The Secret History is Tartt's debut novel, published in 1992. It tells the story of a group of classics students at a small Vermont college who become involved in a murder. The novel is a psychological thriller that explores themes of morality, guilt, and the consequences of intellectual elitism. It's narrated by Richard Papen, who reflects on his time with a group of eccentric, wealthy students studying ancient Greek under an enigmatic professor.

Our Thoughts

We found that The Secret History showed considerable promise in its opening chapters, with an intriguing premise and atmospheric setting that drew us in. However, as the narrative progressed, particularly in the latter half, the story lost much of its initial momentum. The pacing became increasingly sluggish, and what began as an engaging psychological thriller evolved into a laborious read. Despite our initial enthusiasm, we found ourselves struggling to maintain interest and ultimately had to push through to reach the conclusion. While the novel had the foundation for something compelling, it ultimately failed to deliver on its early potential.
